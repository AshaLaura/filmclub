Founder

My name is Asha, and I love movies.

Not just the blockbusters — the weird ones. The surreal ones. The films that feel like someone cracked open their brain and let you wander around inside. City of God. Dark City. Strawberry Mansion. Problemista. The ones that stay with you for days and you just need to talk to someone about them.

The problem is my best friends don't love movies. Not like that.

But I knew somewhere out there, other people did. People who wanted to sit in a dark room together, watch something strange and beautiful, then argue about it over drinks. After years of lockdown and screens and isolation, meeting in real life felt more important than ever — not just convenient, but necessary.

So in October 2023 I started Film Club on Lex, a queer social app in the Bay Area. Over the years about 900 people have joined. But what mattered wasn't the number — it was the 10 to 20 people who actually showed up. Strangers who became regulars. Conversations that kept going long after the credits rolled.

We met almost every other month in 2024. Independent theaters, good bars, real conversations. Our most active year yet. And then it started to feel like work. So I stepped back, reflected, and started building something more sustainable — a real home for Film Club, open to everyone.

If you've ever left a movie wishing you had someone to talk to about it, this is for you.
